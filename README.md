##  Project Overview
This research paper examines how **Artificial Intelligence (AI)** and **Big Data** are transforming governance in India. It focuses on **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI)**, environmental sustainability, and future-ready governance, while addressing challenges of privacy, bias, and regulation.

##  Key Highlights
- **Digital Public Infrastructure (DPI):** Aadhaar, UPI, DigiLocker as AI-enabled platforms.
- **Policy Frameworks:** National Strategy for AI (NSAI), India AI Mission, Digital Personal Data Protection Act (DPDP Act).
- **Governance Applications:** Agriculture (Saagu Baagu), healthcare (ABDM), smart cities, linguistic inclusion (BHASHINI).
- **Regulatory Principles:** Seven Sutras – Trust, People First, Fairness, Accountability, Explainability, Safety, Innovation.
- **Computational Sovereignty:** IndiaAI GPU Network (38,000 GPUs) and AIKosh dataset platform (5,500+ datasets).
- **Economic Impact:** AI projected to add **$1.7 trillion to GDP by 2035**.
- **Case Studies:** Aadhaar privacy debates, Aarogya Setu app, predictive policing in smart cities.

##  Research Objectives
1. Assess AI’s role in strengthening governance.
2. Analyse applications in public administration.
3. Evaluate efficiency, transparency, inclusiveness.
4. Identify ethical challenges.
5. Recommend sustainable policy measures.

##  Methodology
- **Data Sources:** Government reports, academic journals, industry studies, international organizations.
- **Approach:** Descriptive and analytical research using qualitative content analysis and comparative policy analysis.
